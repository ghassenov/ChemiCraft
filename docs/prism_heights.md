# Prism Heights — Optics & Light Physics

**Difficulty:** 4 | **Theme:** Purple/indigo sparks, sparkle particles | **Portal unlocks after:** completing all 5 quests

---

## Learning Objectives

- Understand light as electromagnetic radiation with wave-particle duality
- Learn the visible spectrum (380nm–740nm) and its place in the full EM spectrum
- Understand refraction, Snell's Law, and dispersion (prisms split white light)
- Learn how convex and concave lenses focus or diverge light
- Understand additive color theory (RGB) vs subtractive color mixing
- Learn real-world optics applications: telescopes, microscopes, fiber optics, lasers

## Light Puzzle (Click-to-Place Optics Minigame)

A ray optics puzzle where components are placed on a grid to guide beams to targets:

| Level | Challenge | Teaching Goal |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Place a prism to split white light into colors | Introduction to dispersion |
| 2 | Use color filters to isolate specific wavelengths | RGB filtering |
| 3 | Combine lenses and prisms to focus split light | Convergence and focal points |
| 4 | Use filters to selectively block wavelengths | Wavelength-specific manipulation |
| 5 | Split light with a prism then recombine it | Color mixing is reversible |

**Components available (always all 6, no inventory dependency):**
- Prism (splits white light into spectrum)
- Red, Blue, Green filters (block other wavelengths)
- Convex Lens (focuses light to a point)
- White Light Source (emits full-spectrum beam)

**Feedback:** Each [TEST BEAM] press checks correctness. Wrong answers show "✗ Not quite" with a shake. Correct answers flash and play a success sound.
